Accident and Near-Miss Reporting

What constitutes a 'Near Miss'?

When an accident occurs that doesn't result in an injury, or when something happens which, if not attended to could result in an injury, this is a near-miss. Near-misses are often pre-cursors to injury, and as such should be recorded, or even in some cases reported, so that these situations can be resolved without incident.

Please Note; the necessity of recording vs reporting passes no judgement on the severity of an accident or near-miss, it simply marks a distinction between whether an incident is reportable to the HSE/local authority for investigation or not.
